We are given the following information:

- Field A produced **twice as much wheat** as Field B.
- Field C produced **10 tons more** than Field B.
- The **total harvest** from all three fields is **110 tons**.

---

### Step 1: Assign a variable to the unknown quantity

Let’s let:

- $ x $ = the amount of wheat produced by **Field B** (in tons).

Then:

- Field A produced $ 2x $ tons (twice as much as Field B),
- Field C produced $ x + 10 $ tons (10 tons more than Field B).

---

### Step 2: Set up the equation for the total harvest

The total harvest is the sum of the harvests from all three fields:

$$
\text{Field A} + \text{Field B} + \text{Field C} = \text{Total}
$$

$$
2x + x + (x + 10) = 110
$$

---

### Step 3: Simplify the equation

$$
2x + x + x + 10 = 110
$$

$$
4x + 10 = 110
$$

---

### Step 4: Solve for $ x $

$$
4x = 110 - 10 = 100
$$

$$
x = \frac{100}{4} = 25
$$

---

### Step 5: Find the harvest from each field

- Field B: $ x = 25 $ tons
- Field A: $ 2x = 2 \times 25 = 50 $ tons
- Field C: $ x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 $ tons

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

- **Field A**: 50 tons  
- **Field B**: 25 tons  
- **Field C**: 35 tons  
- **Total**: $ 50 + 25 + 35 = 110 $ tons ✅
