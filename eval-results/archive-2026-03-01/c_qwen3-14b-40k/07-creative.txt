Commander Elara Voss floated in the vacuum of space, her helmet reflecting the infinite blackness of the cosmos. The stars, so familiar from Earth, now shimmered with an eerie, unnatural glow. She had been on a solo mission to investigate a mysterious signal from the Orion Arm, but the deeper she traveled, the more the stars seemed to pulse, as if breathing.

Her suit's sensors flickered with static, and the signal—once clear—had now become a whisper, almost mocking. She reached for her comms, but the channel was dead. A cold dread crept up her spine, and the silence of space felt heavier than ever before.

Then she saw them. Shapes moving between the stars, too large to be natural. They were not stars at all. They were eyes. Thousands of them, watching. Her breath came in shallow gasps, the oxygen in her tank suddenly tasting metallic and stale. The ship’s lights flickered, and for a moment, the stars blinked out, leaving her in absolute darkness.

A low hum vibrated through her suit, not from the ship, but from the void itself. It was a sound that resonated in her bones, a deep, ancient frequency that made her teeth ache. She turned, and the stars reappeared—still, but now they were not distant points of light. They were close, too close, and they were not stars. They were faces.

Elara’s hands trembled as she reached for the controls, but her fingers wouldn’t move. Her mind screamed, but no sound escaped her lips. The ship’s systems went dark, one by one, until she was alone, adrift in the silent, watching dark.

The stars were not stars. They were the eyes of something vast, ancient, and waiting. And she had looked into them—and they had looked back.
