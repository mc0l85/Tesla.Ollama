Alright, let's tackle this seating arrangement problem step by step. We have five people: Alice (A), Bob (B), Carol (C), Dave (D), and Eve (E), and we need to determine their positions from left to right (positions 1 to 5) based on the given clues.

### List of Clues:
1. **Bob is not at either end.**
   - So, Bob cannot be in position 1 or 5.
   
2. **Alice is somewhere to the left of Dave.**
   - A must be in a position number less than D's position.
   
3. **Carol is immediately next to Bob.**
   - C and B must be adjacent (either B-C or C-B).
   
4. **Eve is at one of the ends.**
   - E must be in position 1 or 5.
   
5. **There is exactly one person between Alice and Carol.**
   - The positions of A and C must have exactly one person between them. This means their positions differ by 2 (|A - C| = 2).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**Step 1: Determine Eve's Position**
From clue 4, Eve is at one end. So, E is either in position 1 or 5.

**Step 2: Place Bob's Position**
From clue 1, Bob is not at either end, so B can be in positions 2, 3, or 4.

**Step 3: Consider Carol's Position Relative to Bob**
From clue 3, Carol is immediately next to Bob. So, if B is in position x, then C is in x-1 or x+1 (but must be within 1-5).

**Step 4: Consider Alice and Carol's Positions**
From clue 5, there's exactly one person between Alice and Carol, so |A - C| = 2.

**Step 5: Combine Clues to Find Possible Arrangements**

Let's explore possible positions for B and C first, then place A and E accordingly.

**Case 1: B is in position 2**
- Then C must be in 1 or 3.
  - If C is in 1:
    - Then |A - C| = 2 ⇒ A is in 3 (since C is in 1, A must be 1+2=3 or 1-2=-1, invalid). So A is in 3.
    - Now, positions: 1=C, 2=B, 3=A, then D and E left.
    - E must be at an end (1 or 5), but 1 is taken by C, so E must be in 5.
    - So D is in 4.
    - Check clue 2: A (3) is left of D (4) - OK.
    - Arrangement: C, B, A, D, E
    - Check all clues:
      - B not at ends: B is in 2 - OK
      - A left of D: 3 < 4 - OK
      - C next to B: B=2, C=1 - OK
      - E at end: E=5 - OK
      - One person between A and C: A=3, C=1, difference is 2 - OK
    - This arrangement works.

  - If C is in 3:
    - Then |A - C| = 2 ⇒ A is in 1 or 5.
      - If A is in 1:
        - Then E must be at end, but 1 is taken by A, so E must be in 5.
