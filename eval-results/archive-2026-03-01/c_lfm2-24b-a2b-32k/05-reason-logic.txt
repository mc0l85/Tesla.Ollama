Let's solve the problem step by step.

**Step 1: Define Variables**

Let’s denote:
- \( B \) = amount of wheat (in tons) produced by Field B.

Then:
- Field A produced twice as much as Field B: \( A = 2B \).
- Field C produced 10 tons more than Field B: \( C = B + 10 \).

**Step 2: Write the Total Harvest Equation**

The total harvest from all three fields is 110 tons:
\[
A + B + C = 110
\]

Substitute \( A \) and \( C \) with the expressions in terms of \( B \):
\[
2B + B + (B + 10) = 110
\]

**Step 3: Simplify the Equation**

Combine like terms:
\[
2B + B + B + 10 = 110
\]
\[
4B + 10 = 110
\]

**Step 4: Solve for \( B \)**

Subtract 10 from both sides:
\[
4B = 100
\]

Divide both sides by 4:
\[
B = 25
\]

**Step 5: Find \( A \) and \( C \)**

Now, substitute \( B = 25 \) back into the expressions for \( A \) and \( C \):
\[
A = 2B = 2 \times 25 = 50
\]
\[
C = B + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35
\]

**Step 6: Verify the Solution**

Check that the sum is 110 tons:
\[
A + B + C = 50 + 25 + 35 = 110
\]
The total matches, so the solution is correct.

**Final Answer:**
\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\text{Field A} &= 50 \text{ tons} \\
\text{Field B} &= 25 \text{ tons} \\
\text{Field C} &= 35 \text{ tons}
\end{aligned}
}
\]
