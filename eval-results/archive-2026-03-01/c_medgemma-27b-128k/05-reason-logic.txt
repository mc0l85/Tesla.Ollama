Okay, let's break down this problem step-by-step.

**1. Define Variables:**

*   Let 'x' represent the amount of wheat produced by Field B (in tons).
*   Field A produced twice as much as Field B, so Field A produced '2x' tons.
*   Field C produced 10 tons more than Field B, so Field C produced 'x + 10' tons.

**2. Set up the Equation:**

*   The total harvest is the sum of the production from all three fields: Field A + Field B + Field C = Total Harvest
*   Substitute the variables: 2x + x + (x + 10) = 110

**3. Solve the Equation:**

*   Combine like terms: 4x + 10 = 110
*   Subtract 10 from both sides: 4x = 100
*   Divide both sides by 4: x = 25

**4. Calculate Production for Each Field:**

*   Field B: x = 25 tons
*   Field A: 2x = 2 * 25 = 50 tons
*   Field C: x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 tons

**5. Verify the Solution:**

*   Total Harvest = Field A + Field B + Field C
*   Total Harvest = 50 + 25 + 35 = 110 tons. This matches the given total.

**Answer:**

*   Field A produced 50 tons of wheat.
*   Field B produced 25 tons of wheat.
*   Field C produced 35 tons of wheat.
