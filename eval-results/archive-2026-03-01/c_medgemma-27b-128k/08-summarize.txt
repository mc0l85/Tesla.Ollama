*   **CRISPR-Cas9 is a revolutionary gene editing tool:** Derived from a bacterial defense system, it allows precise DNA cutting at targeted locations, enabling applications across agriculture, medicine (e.g., sickle cell disease therapy), and research.
*   **CRISPR has broad applications but raises ethical concerns:** While offering immense potential, especially in treating diseases, its use, particularly germline editing (altering inherited traits), is controversial and requires careful regulation due to potential unforeseen consequences and societal implications.
*   **Despite ethical debates, CRISPR technology is rapidly advancing and commercially promising:** Significant investment in CRISPR-based companies indicates strong belief in its therapeutic potential, despite ongoing discussions about responsible use and regulation highlighted by events like the He Jiankui case.
