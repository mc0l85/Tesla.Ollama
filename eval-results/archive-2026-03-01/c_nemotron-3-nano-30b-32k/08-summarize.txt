- CRISPR-Cas9, derived from a bacterial immune system, was adapted for precise DNA editing, earning Doudna and Charpentier the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.  
- The technology has enabled breakthroughs in agriculture, medicine (e.g., FDA-approved sickle cell disease therapy in 2023), and scientific research, though ethical concerns remain, especially around heritable germline modifications.  
- Despite ethical debates, CRISPR investment surged past $5 billion in 2023, reflecting strong commercial confidence in its therapeutic and biotechnological applications.
