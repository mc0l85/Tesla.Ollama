Let Field B's production be x tons.

Step 1: Express the production of the other fields in terms of x.
  Field A produces twice as much as Field B, so Field A = 2x.
  Field C produces 10 tons more than Field B, so Field C = x + 10.

Step 2: Write an equation for the total harvest.
  Total production = Field A + Field B + Field C = 2x + x + (x + 10) = 110

Step 3: Simplify the equation.
  2x + x + x + 10 = 4x + 10 = 110

Step 4: Solve for x.
  4x + 10 = 110
  Subtract 10 from both sides: 4x = 100
  Divide by 4: x = 25

Step 5: Determine the production for each field.
  Field B = x = 25 tons
  Field A = 2x = 2 × 25 = 50 tons
  Field C = x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 tons

Thus, the fields produced:
  Field A: 50 tons
  Field B: 25 tons
  Field C: 35 tons
