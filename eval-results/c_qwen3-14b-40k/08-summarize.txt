- CRISPR-Cas9, originally a bacterial immune system, was reprogrammed by Doudna and Charpentier to edit DNA precisely, earning them the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.  
- CRISPR has been applied in agriculture, medicine (e.g., FDA-approved sickle cell therapy), and basic research, showcasing its broad impact and therapeutic potential.  
- Ethical concerns, especially around germline editing, have emerged, highlighted by the controversial He Jiankui case, though CRISPR continues to attract significant commercial investment.
