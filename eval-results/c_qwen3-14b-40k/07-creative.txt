Commander Elara Voss drifted in the silent void, her breath fogging the helmet of her suit as she floated near the edge of the *Odyssey* spacecraft. The stars ahead shimmered like diamonds scattered across a black velvet curtain. She had seen them a thousand times, but tonight, something was different. They pulsed—slowly, rhythmically, as if they were breathing.

Her gloved fingers tightened around the rail. The ship’s sensors had picked up strange energy readings, but Elara had dismissed them as anomalies. Now, as she stared into the abyss, she felt a cold dread creep up her spine. The stars weren’t stars. They were eyes—hundreds of them, watching, waiting.

A low hum resonated through the void, vibrating in her bones. Her helmet’s visor flickered, and for a moment, the stars blurred into faces—distorted, ancient, and hungry. She turned away, but the darkness was no longer empty. It was alive.

Back inside the *Odyssey*, the ship’s systems flickered erratically. The lights dimmed, then brightened, as if the ship itself was breathing in sync with the stars. Elara’s pulse pounded in her ears as she rushed to the control panel. The monitors displayed impossible data—coordinates that didn’t exist, temperatures that defied logic, and a signal repeating in every language she had ever known: *“We are here.”*

A voice, not from the speakers but from deep within her mind, whispered, *“You are not alone.”*

The ship shuddered violently. Alarms blared, but Elara couldn’t move. The stars had opened their eyes fully now, and she saw the truth: they weren’t stars. They were sentinels—watching, waiting for the moment to reclaim what had been lost.

As the *Odyssey* drifted toward the unseen horizon, Elara felt her body unravel, her thoughts dissolving into the void. The stars had always been watching. And now, they had found her.
