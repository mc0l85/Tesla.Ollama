We are given the following information:

- Field A produced **twice as much wheat as Field B**.
- Field C produced **10 tons more than Field B**.
- The **total harvest from all three fields was 110 tons**.

---

### Step 1: Assign a variable to the unknown quantity
Let the amount of wheat produced by **Field B** be:

$$
x \text{ tons}
$$

---

### Step 2: Express the production of the other fields in terms of $ x $

- **Field A**: $ 2x $ (twice as much as Field B)
- **Field C**: $ x + 10 $ (10 tons more than Field B)

---

### Step 3: Set up the equation for the total harvest

$$
\text{Field A} + \text{Field B} + \text{Field C} = 110
$$

Substitute the expressions:

$$
2x + x + (x + 10) = 110
$$

---

### Step 4: Simplify the equation

$$
2x + x + x + 10 = 110
$$
$$
4x + 10 = 110
$$

---

### Step 5: Solve for $ x $

$$
4x = 110 - 10
$$
$$
4x = 100
$$
$$
x = 25
$$

---

### Step 6: Find the production of each field

- **Field B**: $ x = 25 $ tons
- **Field A**: $ 2x = 2 \times 25 = 50 $ tons
- **Field C**: $ x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 $ tons

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

- **Field A**: **50 tons**
- **Field B**: **25 tons**
- **Field C**: **35 tons**
