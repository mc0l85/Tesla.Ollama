Here's how we can solve this problem step-by-step:

**1. Define Variables:**

* Let's use 'x' to represent the amount of wheat produced by Field B.
* Field A produced twice as much as Field B, so Field A = 2x
* Field C produced 10 tons more than Field B, so Field C = x + 10

**2. Set up the Equation:**

The total harvest was 110 tons. So:
Field A + Field B + Field C = 110
2x + x + (x + 10) = 110

**3. Solve for x:**

* Combine like terms: 4x + 10 = 110
* Subtract 10 from both sides: 4x = 100
* Divide both sides by 4: x = 25

**4. Calculate Production for Each Field:**

* **Field B:** x = 25 tons
* **Field A:** 2x = 2 * 25 = 50 tons
* **Field C:** x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 tons

**Answer:**

* Field A produced 50 tons of wheat.
* Field B produced 25 tons of wheat.
* Field C produced 35 tons of wheat.

**Check:** 50 + 25 + 35 = 110 (This confirms our answer is correct!)
