To determine how many tons each field produced, we begin by defining the amount produced by **Field B** as a variable. Let:

- Field B = $ x $ tons

From the problem, we know:

- **Field A** produced **twice as much** as Field B → Field A = $ 2x $
- **Field C** produced **10 tons more** than Field B → Field C = $ x + 10 $

---

### Step 1: Write the Total Harvest Equation

The total harvest from all three fields is given as 110 tons. So:

$$
\text{Field A} + \text{Field B} + \text{Field C} = 110
$$

Substitute the expressions in terms of $ x $:

$$
2x + x + (x + 10) = 110
$$

Simplify the left-hand side:

$$
2x + x + x + 10 = 4x + 10
$$

So:

$$
4x + 10 = 110
$$

---

### Step 2: Solve for $ x $

Subtract 10 from both sides:

$$
4x = 100
$$

Divide both sides by 4:

$$
x = 25
$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate Each Field's Production

- **Field B** = $ x = 25 $ tons  
- **Field A** = $ 2x = 2 \times 25 = 50 $ tons  
- **Field C** = $ x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 $ tons  

---

### Step 4: Verify the Total

$$
50 + 25 + 35 = 110 \quad \text{✓}
$$

All conditions are satisfied.

---

### ✅ Final Answer

- **Field A** produced $\boxed{50}$ tons  
- **Field B** produced $\boxed{25}$ tons  
- **Field C** produced $\boxed{35}$ tons
