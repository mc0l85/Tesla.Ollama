The coffee in Commander Aris’s cup was cold, a mundane anchor in the silent void. Outside the *Odyssey*’s viewport, the stars glittered with their familiar, distant promise. But tonight, they *hissed*.

Aris adjusted the spectrometer, expecting the usual spectral lines. Instead, the display flickered, spitting out impossible data: stellar radiation *taste* of ozone and copper. She touched the viewport glass. It wasn’t cold glass, but *wrong* cold, like ice formed from silence, seeping into her bones. The light wasn’t reflected; it *pulsed*, deep within the glass, pulling at her eyes.

Then, she saw it. Not points of light, but *mouths*. Tiny, dark orifices in the fabric of space, opening and closing with a soundless, wet sigh. The “stars” weren’t distant suns; they were *gaping*. They weren’t illuminating the void—they were *consuming* it, drawing the light, the very *idea* of light, into their hungry centers. The constellations weren’t patterns; they were *tears* in the sky, weeping blackness.

Aris stumbled back, her breath ragged. The coffee cup slipped from her fingers, shattering on the deck.
