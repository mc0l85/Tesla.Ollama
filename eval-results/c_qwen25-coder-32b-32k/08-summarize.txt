- CRISPR-Cas9 technology was repurposed by Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier to edit DNA at specific locations, earning them the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
- CRISPR has been applied in agriculture to create disease-resistant crops, in medicine for therapies like sickle cell disease treatment, and in basic research to study gene functions.
- Ethical concerns over germline editing and commercial investments exceeding $5 billion in 2023 highlight both the challenges and potential of CRISPR technology.
