In the vast expanse of space, Captain Elara Myles gazed out from the porthole, her breath frosting the glass. The stars, once twinkling beacons of hope, now seemed to pulse with an eerie, unnatural light. She had always marveled at their serene glow, but tonight, they felt like eyes watching her.

The ship's AI, a monotone voice that had become her constant companion, announced another malfunction. Elara sighed, adjusting her helmet to block the cold, biting air. She had been alone for months, her crew long dead or lost in the void. The stars were her only companions, or so she thought.

She ventured into the observation deck, the hum of the life support system a lullaby that did little to soothe her growing unease. As she approached the central window, the stars seemed closer, their light more intense. She reached out, fingers tingling as if they touched something intangible.

Suddenly, the ship lurched violently. Elara clung to the rail, her heart pounding. Through the chaos, she saw the stars—no longer distant suns but swirling, malevolent entities. They seemed to reach out, tendrils of light wrapping around the ship like a ghostly net.

In a panic, she tried to communicate with Earth, but only static filled her helmet. The stars closed in, their light blinding. Elara screamed, her voice lost in the void. When the light faded, she was alone, the stars now silent sentinels of an unknown horror.
