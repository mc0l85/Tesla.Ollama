Let's denote the amount of wheat produced by Field B as \( x \) tons.

According to the problem:
- Field A produced twice as much wheat as Field B, so Field A produced \( 2x \) tons.
- Field C produced 10 tons more than Field B, so Field C produced \( x + 10 \) tons.
- The total harvest from all three fields is 110 tons.

We can set up the following equation to represent the total harvest:
\[ x + 2x + (x + 10) = 110 \]

Simplifying the equation:
\[ 4x + 10 = 110 \]

Subtract 10 from both sides:
\[ 4x = 100 \]

Divide by 4:
\[ x = 25 \]

So, Field B produced 25 tons. Now we can find the production of Fields A and C:
- Field A: \( 2x = 2 \times 25 = 50 \) tons
- Field C: \( x + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 \) tons

Thus, the production for each field is:
- Field A: 50 tons
- Field B: 25 tons
- Field C: 35 tons
