*   CRISPR-Cas9 was reprogrammed in 2012 by Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier, leading to a breakthrough in gene editing and the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
*   The technology has diverse applications in agriculture, medicine, and basic research, though it faces ethical challenges regarding germline editing.
*   Despite ethical concerns and calls for regulation, the technology remains commercially viable with significant investment exceeding $5 billion in 2023.
