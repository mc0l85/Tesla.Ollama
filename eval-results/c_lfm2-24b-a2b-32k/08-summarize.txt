- **Scientific Breakthrough**: CRISPR-Cas9, originally a bacterial defense mechanism, was repurposed by Doudna and Charpentier (2020 Nobel Prize) to enable precise DNA editing, transforming research and medicine.  
- **Diverse Applications & Ethical Challenges**: Used in agriculture, FDA-approved therapies (e.g., sickle cell disease), and gene function studies, but raises ethical concerns, especially over heritable germline editing.  
- **Regulatory & Commercial Dynamics**: The 2018 He Jiankui scandal spurred calls for stricter oversight, yet 2023 saw $5B+ in startup investments, reflecting strong commercial confidence in CRISPR’s therapeutic potential.
