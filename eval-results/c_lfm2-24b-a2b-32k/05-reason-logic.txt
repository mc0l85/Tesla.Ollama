Let's solve the problem step by step.

**Let’s define the variables:**
- Let \( B \) represent the amount of wheat (in tons) produced by **Field B**.
- According to the problem:
  - **Field A** produced twice as much as Field B: \( A = 2B \).
  - **Field C** produced 10 tons more than Field B: \( C = B + 10 \).

**The total harvest is 110 tons:**
\[
A + B + C = 110
\]

**Substitute the expressions for \( A \) and \( C \) in terms of \( B \):**
\[
2B + B + (B + 10) = 110
\]

**Combine like terms:**
\[
4B + 10 = 110
\]

**Subtract 10 from both sides:**
\[
4B = 100
\]

**Divide both sides by 4:**
\[
B = 25
\]

**Now find \( A \) and \( C \):**
\[
A = 2B = 2 \times 25 = 50
\]
\[
C = B + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35
\]

**Final Answer:**
\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\text{Field A} &= 50 \text{ tons} \\
\text{Field B} &= 25 \text{ tons} \\
\text{Field C} &= 35 \text{ tons}
\end{aligned}
}
\]

**Verification:**
\[
50 + 25 + 35 = 110 \quad \text{(Correct)}
\]

**Answer:**  
Field A produced **50 tons**, Field B produced **25 tons**, and Field C produced **35 tons**.
