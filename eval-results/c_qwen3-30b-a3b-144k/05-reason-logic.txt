To determine how many tons of wheat each field produced, we start by defining variables based on the relationships given in the problem.

---

### Step 1: Define Variables

Let:
- $ B $ = amount of wheat produced by **Field B** (in tons)

From the problem:
- **Field A** produced **twice as much** as Field B → $ A = 2B $
- **Field C** produced **10 tons more** than Field B → $ C = B + 10 $

---

### Step 2: Use Total Harvest to Form an Equation

The total harvest from all three fields is 110 tons:

$$
A + B + C = 110
$$

Substitute the expressions for $ A $ and $ C $ in terms of $ B $:

$$
2B + B + (B + 10) = 110
$$

Simplify:

$$
2B + B + B + 10 = 110 \\
4B + 10 = 110
$$

---

### Step 3: Solve for $ B $

$$
4B = 100 \\
B = \frac{100}{4} = 25
$$

So, **Field B produced 25 tons**.

---

### Step 4: Calculate Production for Fields A and C

- **Field A**: $ A = 2B = 2 \times 25 = 50 $ tons  
- **Field C**: $ C = B + 10 = 25 + 10 = 35 $ tons

---

### Step 5: Verify the Total

$$
A + B + C = 50 + 25 + 35 = 110 \text{ tons}
$$

This matches the given total, confirming our solution is correct.

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

- **Field A**: **50 tons**  
- **Field B**: **25 tons**  
- **Field C**: **35 tons**
